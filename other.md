### (Slightly) interesting facts  

Besides physics, I'm a big linguistics nerd. It turns out natural languages are like algebraic structures, just with fewer proofs and more exceptions. Here are the languages I've learned/am currently learning:

Korean - native language, although my mom told me I do sound like a non-Korean trying to sound Korean; #Spanish# - conversational to intermediate, picked up most of it as a linear combination of Duolingo, 2 years of high school Spanish, and growing up in Arizona. Confident enough to order food in Spanish at a Mexican sit-in; #Mandarin Chinese# - conversational, also took 2 years of high school Mandarin. I forgot how to write in Hanzi (Chinese characters) though, Hanzi itself probably qualifies as a separate mathematical structure; #Russian# - conversational to intermediate, I enjoy listening to a bunch Kino (Кино) songs. If I were ever stranded in Russia, I'd probably survive using the two magic words, 'Хорошо́' and 'что?'; #Norwegian# - elementary, started learning Norwegian because I heard it's a Germanic language with a very similar structure as English & thought it would be easy. Not necessarily easy, but it's definitely interesting.
