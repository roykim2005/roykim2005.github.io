### (Slightly) interesting facts  

Besides physics, I'm a big linguistics nerd. It turns out natural languages are like algebraic structures, just with fewer proofs and more exceptions. Here are the languages I've learned/am currently learning:

**Korean** - Native language, although my mom told me I do sound like a non-Korean trying to sound Korean; **Spanish** - Conversational to intermediate. Picked up most of it as a linear combination of Duolingo, two years of high school Spanish, and growing up in Arizona. Confident enough to order food in Spanish at a Mexican sit-in; **Mandarin Chinese** - Conversational. Also took two years of high school Mandarin. I've forgotten how to write Hanzi (Chinese characters), Hanzi themselves practically qualify as their own mathematical structure; **Russian** - Conversational to intermediate. I enjoy listening to Kino (Кино) songs, and If you haven't heard them, I highly recommend giving their songs a listen. If I were ever stranded in Russia, I'd probably survive on the two magic words, 'Хорошо́' and 'что?'; **Norwegian** - Elementary. Started learning Norwegian because I heard its grammar was very similar to English. Not necessarily easy, but it's definitely interesting.
